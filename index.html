About Me

I love to learn, and use what I learn to create complex, useful software that improves people's lives. I am a full stack software engineer currently working as web developer for Minnesota Thespians, and doing shrink analysis at Canteen One on the Field Support Shrink Team. Last spring I graduated from Prime Digital Academy focusing on their Full Stack Software Engineering program to learn computer science, digital design, and front and back end software engineering practices. I have since focused on developing further skills with JavaScript, C#, Python, Swift, React, React Native, SQL, AWS, iOS, Android, and web design accessibility. I am passionate about well designed, intuitive applications that are beneficial to someone's time online. I am happiest when actively creating, rather than passively observing others working on projects I am interested in. Because of my multidisciplinary career, I continually seek out opportunities to develop my transferrable, personal, and knowledge-based skills to pursue ideas development and design. During my time at Prime Digital Academy, I learned programs and libraries like React.js, C#, SQL, PostgreSQL, Node.js, Express.js, and jQuery, and tested code on Postman and Jest. We coded a full CRUD web application for our solo projects to test acquired coding comprehension, and a larger group project for an external client to use for B2C interaction. I was grateful to be chosen as team lead for the client project which was building a two sided marketplace similar to AirBnB. We integrated APIs such as AWS S3, Google Places, and Google Directions for component functionality and it exceeded expectations. It required extensive self-led learning, and was also a great lesson in Git commands through trial and error! For personal project practice I have worked with Notion, Spotify, and Google Maps APIs on web and mobile programming, and for application deployment I have practiced with Heroku, AWS, and MongoDB. Learning new technologies brings me a sense of accomplishment and joy, just like my 1,419 day streak from learning new languages on Duolingo. When I've closed my laptop for the day I am most likely hiking in a Minnesota Stake Park, cooking for my family, taking portraits of my friends, attending a concert/musical/opera, or learning about birds.
